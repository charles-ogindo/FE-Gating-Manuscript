## 2026-06-01/02 — Pipeline validation by reference-ligand back-docking

**Claim**: We validated the receptor-preparation and box-computation pipeline by back-docking the reference ligand (paclitaxel, TA1) into its native binding site on β-tubulin (PDB 1JFF). Vina (v1.2.5, exhaustiveness=64, single-threaded) scored the recovered pose at -8.6 kcal/mol, with non-binding controls (aspirin and juliprosopine) scoring -5.3 and -6.2 kcal/mol respectively. The ~2-3 kcal/mol selectivity margin confirms proper pocket localization.

**Source**: job 4a37bb0c-7655-411e-a238-1ff5b7bab910 (multi-ligand dock); commits 85d0878 (OpenBabel receptor pipeline), 71545f6 (serial renumber), cce78ed (box-chain-pick fix).

**Caveat**: Run used exhaustiveness=64 (subsequently reduced to 32 in commit 1a87692 to balance search depth with throughput). Repeating the back-dock at exhaustiveness=32 is a useful confirmation but not yet performed.

## 2026-06-02 — Pipeline bug discovery and remediation

**Claim**: During development, two latent bugs in the docking pipeline were identified and corrected. (1) Receptor preparation: PDB→PDBQT conversion via Meeko (and MGLTools previously) failed on PDBFixer-output structures due to distance-based bond perception inferring spurious backbone bonds (CA-to-carbonyl-O bonds at ~1.7-1.8 Å, falling within rdDetermineBonds.DetermineConnectivity's cutoff). We replaced this stage with an OpenBabel pipeline including metal split-charge-remerge handling for transition metals (Zn, Mg, Ca, Mn, Fe, Cu, Na, K) that OpenBabel's Gasteiger implementation cannot parameterize directly. (2) Docking box: the prior box-computation code used a "smallest chain by atom count" heuristic to identify the reference ligand, which is correct for peptide ligands (their own chain) but fails for small molecules embedded as HETATMs within a protein chain (the standard PDB CCD convention; e.g., TA1 in chain B residue 601 of 1JFF). We replaced the heuristic with explicit ligand identity (chain, residue number, insertion code) supplied via the user-facing form. Results from runs prior to commit cce78ed against holo small-molecule references used an incorrectly-sized search volume (~19× larger than the corrected box for the tubulin-paclitaxel system) and should be re-run for any methodological claim depending on pose localization.

**Source**: commits 85d0878 (OpenBabel receptor pipeline), 71545f6 (atom serial renumber), cce78ed (ligand-identity-based box), 0519194 (N-terminal hydrogen normalization, retained but not the load-bearing fix), backlog items 10 + 12.

**Methodological note for future-self / co-authors**: prior results from jobs 3e4c8a16, 36109566, 5ba07bd4, 8305ee45 (since retired as failed in PG via item-10+12 close-out) used incorrect search volumes. Job d96d719a was marked "MD pipeline proven end-to-end" in earlier project memory but its dock results used the same incorrect box; that claim should be re-evaluated against corrected pipeline.

## 2026-06-02 — Pocket-detection validation against published structural literature

**Claim**: The geometric pocket-detection layer (detect_pockets in backend/app/analysis/pockets.py, configured with contact_cutoff=5.0 Å against the bound reference ligand) recovers the canonical taxane binding site on β-tubulin (PDB 1JFF). For the holo reference structure with paclitaxel (TA1) bound, the algorithm identified 22 chain-B residues lining the binding cavity, including HIS229 and LEU230 (M-loop), LEU217 (H7 helix face), and GLU22 / VAL23 / ASP26 / GLU27 / ASP226. These match residues independently documented in published structural studies of the taxane site (cite as appropriate: Löwe et al. 2001 [1JFF structure]; Snyder et al. 2001 and Nogales et al. 1998 for the taxane site characterization). No alpha-tubulin residues were detected, consistent with the established biology that taxanes bind exclusively to β-tubulin.

**Source**: job 07996556-4193-48a6-8786-315996e91811 (canonical evidence); commit 79fcbd9 (detect_pockets wired into classic flow + backfill); pocket.json with pocket_id=P1, centroid=(-0.399, -16.403, 14.621), volume=1196.6 Å³, source="bound_ligand_envelope", confidence=0.95.

**Visualization layer**: commit c504de2 added a translucent molecular surface (Solvent-Excluded Surface, probeRadius=1.4 Å, alpha=0.30) rendered via Mol*'s native SES around the detected pocket residues. The surface composes with the existing residue-stick overlay (yellow sticks at the same residue set) and with docked pose rendering, allowing visual assessment of how docked poses occupy the actual binding pocket geometry rather than merely the rectangular search volume. No external pocket-detection tooling (fpocket, MSMS) required.

**Caveat**: The detection uses bound-ligand-envelope source — i.e., it detects the pocket FROM the ligand's location, rather than predicting it ab initio. For apo structures, detect_pockets_apo provides a probe-grid burial heuristic for cavity discovery; that path was not exercised in this validation.

**Methodological note**: For docking-validation purposes, the residue list provides chemistry-actionable information ("which residues should the ligand interact with?") that complements the geometric box.json. Subsequent docking studies can compute per-pose contact maps (via backend/app/utils/interactions.py: detect_hydrogen_bonds, detect_hydrophobic_contacts, detect_pi_stacking, detect_salt_bridges) against this residue set for selectivity reasoning.

## 2026-06-02 — Multi-ligand selectivity validation and results-export tooling

**Claim**: Following the close of items 10 + 12 (receptor preparation and box-computation bug fixes), a multi-ligand docking experiment was conducted against the corrected pipeline to assess affinity discrimination across structurally distinct compounds. Three ligands were docked into the tubulin-paclitaxel binding site of β-tubulin (PDB 1JFF; search box centered on the TA1 reference ligand): paclitaxel itself (back-dock of the reference ligand), juliprosopine (a polycyclic natural product without known tubulin affinity), and primaquine (an antimalarial without known tubulin affinity). Vina scoring (v1.2.5, exhaustiveness=64, single-threaded; values predating the runtime-defaults reduction in commit 1a87692) produced binding affinities of -8.681 kcal/mol (paclitaxel), -6.201 kcal/mol (juliprosopine), and -6.657 kcal/mol (primaquine). The 2.0–2.5 kcal/mol selectivity gap between the reference ligand and the non-binding controls is consistent with proper pocket localization and chemistry-meaningful affinity discrimination on the corrected pipeline. The selectivity margin is the methodologically meaningful signal; the absolute paclitaxel back-dock score (-8.6 kcal/mol) is consistent with affinities reported in published Vina docking studies of this system.

**Source**: job 4a37bb0c-7655-411e-a238-1ff5b7bab910 (multi-ligand dock executed via SPA-driven workflow); commits cce78ed (box-chain-pick fix), 85d0878 (OpenBabel receptor pipeline), 71545f6 (atom serial renumbering), 79fcbd9 (pocket detection wired into classic flow).

**Results-export tooling**: SPA frontend now provides client-side Download CSV and Download JSON affordances for dock results (commit fb250b0; item 14d), addressing prior friction where users had to extract dock_scores.json from disk manually. CSV emits the displayed results state (ligand_id, score, status, smiles, rank) with RFC 4180 quoting; JSON fetches the canonical dock_scores.json artifact on click (full scenario-aware shape including engine, weights, and per-pose provenance). Filename pattern: dock_results_<jobId-short>_<UTC-YYYY-MM-DD>.{csv,json}.

**Caveat**: The 4a37bb0c run used exhaustiveness=64 (subsequently lowered to 32 in commit 1a87692; total CPU budget unchanged at 4 cores via DOCKING_VINA_CPU=2 × DOCKING_MAX_PARALLEL=2). Repeating the selectivity comparison at exhaustiveness=32 is a useful confirmation but not yet performed; the affinity values reported here should be considered the validation point for the bug-fixed pipeline rather than for the production defaults.

## 2026-06-03 — MD validation of taxol docked pose (corrected)

**Claim**: The Vina-docked rank-1 pose of paclitaxel against β-tubulin (PDB 1JFF) was subjected to 500 ps of implicit-solvent (obc2) molecular dynamics at 300 K with amber14 + GAFF-2.11 force fields. The ligand remained in the canonical taxane binding site throughout the trajectory: ligand center-of-mass remained within 1–4 Å of the pocket center across all 101 sampled frames; 15 binding-site residues maintained contact at every frame; H-bond persistence was 100%; receptor-aligned ligand RMSD ≤2.5 Å throughout, ending at 1.5 Å. Backbone Cα RMSD plateaued at 3.1 ± 0.1 Å after 150 ps with a late-trajectory drift slope of −0.0015 Å/ps (effectively zero), consistent with normal equilibrium fluctuation for an 877-residue dimer in implicit solvent. The pose is supported by MD on the validated timescale.

**Source**: job 1f01da83-ac18-4103-8631-2053873967c0 (taxol MD validation; 500 ps production at 5 ps stride; 101 frames); commit (THIS COMMIT SHA — fixes methodology bug in analyze.py).

**Methodology correction**: An initial analysis run reported "ligand RMSD = 4.94 Å (drifting)" — this was a methodology artifact in `backend/app/md/analyze.py` where the `_ligand_heavy_coords` function classified all HETATM records as "ligand," sweeping up bound cofactors (GTP, GDP) and metal ions (Zn²⁺, Mg²⁺) along with the docked taxol. The combined-cloud Kabsch RMSD was meaningless because each component moves on an independent rigid-body trajectory. The fix (this commit) restricts the ligand classification to HETATMs that are not cofactors/metals/waters (via shared lookup tables with the receptor-prep pipeline: `_METAL_IONS` and `_COFACTOR_SMILES` from `md_receptor_prep.py`). Corrected taxol-only metrics: `rmsd_ligand_final_a = 0.65 Å` (was 4.94 Å); `rmsd_ligand_max_a = 1.30 Å` (was 10.82 Å); `rmsd_backbone_final_a = 3.14 Å` (unchanged — receptor parsing was not affected). Verdict thresholds were also re-tuned to standard MD conventions (stable ≤ 2.0 Å, drifting 2-4 Å, unstable > 4.0 Å), driven primarily by ligand RMSD; backbone now informs the rationale text but does not independently downgrade an otherwise-stable pose. Verdict reclassified from "drifting" to "stable".

**Caveat**: Single-replicate run; would benefit from 3–5 independent replicates with different seeds for stronger statistical support. Free energy estimation (MM-GBSA / FEP) deferred to future work. Implicit-solvent obc2 produces somewhat-higher backbone RMSDs than explicit-solvent runs; future work could run in TIP3P + PME for stricter validation. Original buggy analysis files preserved alongside corrected versions as `md/rmsd.csv.buggy`, `md/hbonds.csv.buggy`, `md/contacts.csv.buggy`, `md/summary.json.buggy` for forensic comparison.

## 2026-06-16 — MM-GBSA binding-free-energy estimates on FE-gate-qualifying MD runs

**Claim**: Single-trajectory MM-GBSA estimates produced on the five MD runs that cleared the corrected stability gate (`docs/all_md_corrected_gate.md`) discriminate the known taxane-pocket binder (paclitaxel) from a structurally distinct non-binding control (juliprosopine) by approximately 36 kcal/mol on the same β-tubulin pocket and force-field stack. Explicit-TIP3P 1 ns MD of paclitaxel in three top-ranked Vina poses produced ΔG_bind = −58.55 ± 0.39, −56.58 ± 0.53, and −51.41 ± 0.70 kcal/mol (mean ± SEM, n=60 frames per run from the last-30 % equilibrated window). Juliprosopine docked into the same pocket, MD'd under the same protocol, produced ΔG_bind = −20.32 ± 0.33 kcal/mol (n=60). Component partition (single-trajectory force-group dispatch: bonded ≈ 0 by construction, nonbonded + GBSA solvation): paclitaxel poses showed nonbonded contributions of −104 to −121 kcal/mol partially offset by solvation penalties of +48 to +64 kcal/mol; juliprosopine showed roughly half the magnitude of both components (nonbonded −42, solvation +22), consistent with its smaller heavy-atom footprint (42 vs 113) and lower top-5 residue-contact persistence in the equilibrated window (81 % vs 99 % for paclitaxel). The ~36 kcal/mol ΔG_bind selectivity margin is the methodologically meaningful signal — it complements the −2.5 kcal/mol Vina-score selectivity already established in the 2026-06-02 multi-ligand validation against the same pocket on the same docking job.

**Source**: jobs `5bc61f59-834f-4e71-a492-d32ddfdc7326`, `34840aa1-cbf5-4c6b-a665-ea4f52110f5d`, `80e53d8a-1926-4525-b2e1-55cb1e30eedd` (paclitaxel poses 0/1/2 explicit TIP3P), `a0b04941-e4b0-40ef-9459-67becac4a61c` (juliprosopine pose 0 explicit TIP3P) — all derived from docking job `4a37bb0c-7655-411e-a238-1ff5b7bab910`; commits `117e0cd` (engine fixes: frame_000-sourced topology + GAFF cache reuse + shared SystemGenerator + last-30 % eq window) and THIS COMMIT (sweep output `docs/free_energy_qualifying.md` + publication-notes entry); estimator at `backend/app/free_energy/mmgbsa.py:estimate_mmgbsa`; runner at `backend/app/free_energy/mmgbsa_runner.py:compute_md_fe`; force-field stack amber14-all + amber14/tip3p ion templates + implicit/obc2 + gaff-2.11 (reuses the MD parameterization).

**Methodology note**: Configurational entropy (normal-mode / quasi-harmonic) is OMITTED; the reported ΔG is the enthalpic + solvation part — the standard MM-GBSA quantity in the literature. MM-GBSA was applied as a single-trajectory subtraction (ΔG = ⟨E_complex⟩ − ⟨E_receptor⟩ − ⟨E_ligand⟩) with all three systems built consistently against the same force-field stack — bonded terms cancel by construction. Frames were taken from the last 30 % of the production trajectory (n=60 for explicit, 61 for implicit) — matching the equilibrated window the stability gate qualified each run on. The bundled gate at `backend/app/free_energy/gating.py` marks all five runs `sampling_adequate: false / preliminary: true` because it keys on `summary.verdict == "stable"` and all five carry `verdict == "drifting"`. That older gate conflates drift from the docked pose with current instability and is superseded for qualification purposes by the corrected gate at `docs/all_md_corrected_gate.md`, which judges convergence + current state and ignores the verdict; the FE numbers above are reported under the corrected-gate authority.

**Solvent-stack quantification (auxiliary)**: A fifth FE run was computed on `02f30602-8624-4c0a-863b-9c130d357364` (paclitaxel pose 0, Run A extended, IMPLICIT obc2 1 ns) and produced ΔG_bind = −43.40 ± 0.66 kcal/mol (n=61). Same pose, same force-field stack, same MD timescale — the only deliberate difference vs `5bc61f59` is the MD solvent (implicit obc2 vs explicit TIP3P). The 15 kcal/mol gap is the explicit-vs-implicit solvent-stack effect made quantitative on this pocket and supports the prior strategic decision (recorded 2026-06-16 in `docs/all_md_corrected_gate.md`) to declare implicit-solvent MD a declined path for FE-grade work. This result is reported as an evidence/calibration data point — NOT as an FE candidate for paclitaxel binding.

**Caveat**: Single-replicate per pose; would benefit from 3–5 independent replicates (different seeds, optionally different starting velocities) for stronger statistical support. Intra-run SEM (~0.3–0.7 kcal/mol) is much tighter than typical between-replicate MM-GBSA variability in the literature and should NOT be quoted as the overall uncertainty bound; the inter-replicate σ for tubulin-class systems is more commonly 2–5 kcal/mol, which would reduce the paclitaxel-vs-juliprosopine selectivity confidence interval but not the rank-order signal. The MM-GBSA estimator is widely used as a relative-affinity tool rather than an absolute-affinity predictor — the ~36 kcal/mol selectivity is the supported claim; the absolute ΔG_bind values for taxol (~−55 kcal/mol) are at the upper end of the typical MM-GBSA-on-tubulin range reported in the literature and should be interpreted as relative numbers within this sweep, not as predictions of absolute binding affinity. The AM1-BCC charge derivation backend (AmberTools / OpenEye) is NOT registered in the runtime env; all five runs reused the persisted GAFF template cache that the MD engine wrote at MD time, so the AM1-BCC parameterization underlying these results is the one produced by the MD pipeline (a reuse, not a fresh re-derivation).
